Stay Safe Online. Prevention Checklist for College Students

1. When you make a password for something make sure it is really strong and one of a kind. It should have a lot of characters like letters and numbers and symbols so it is hard for someone to guess.

2. You should turn on something called 2-Factor Authentication on your email and banking and social media apps. This is called 2FA for short.

3. Do not ever share some codes like OTPs or PINs or your bank details with someone over WhatsApp or SMS or email.

4. When you want to get an app only get it from the Google Play Store or the App Store. Do not click on links from places to get apps.

5. If you get an email or a message with a link in it be careful. Only click on the link if you know who sent it to you.

6. You should update the software on your phone and your apps all the time. This helps keep you safe from people who want to hurt you online.

7. When you use UPI to send money make sure you are sending it to the person. Check their name first. It is also an idea to start with a small amount of money.

8. If someone tries to trick you with UPI you should call 1930 away. This is the National Cyber Crime Helpline.

9. If someone tries to scam you with money you should call your bank away within one hour and you should also go to cybercrime.gov.in to make a complaint.

10. Never share your Aadhaar or PAN details. Take a picture of them and send it to someone you do not know online. Stay safe online by following these rules, for college students.
